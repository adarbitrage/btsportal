Title: What To Do If Campaign and Banners Turn Off Before $1,500 Spend

In this video, I'll show you what to do if all of your banners turn inactive and your campaign stops before you reach $1,500 in total spend. This situation can occur if you turned off some banners early or if certain banners undershot their spending target. For example, if you have 15 banners, each should ideally spend $100, but some may not reach that target before they deactivate.

To address this, you'll need to reactivate the banners you want to continue running to utilize the remaining budget. In my case, there’s a large banner that I don’t expect to perform well, as the others with similar messaging received clicks but didn’t convert. Since my overall spend is low, I can’t turn this banner off yet. If your banners are already inactive and your spend is below $1,500, this may have happened automatically. You’ll need to ensure at least one banner remains active.

If your campaign has already turned off, go to your basic settings tab. In the basic info section, check if the toggle has flipped off automatically. Even if you haven’t reached $1,500 in spend, reactivate your best-performing banners and toggle the campaign back on. In my case, I’m reactivating the banners that performed well to continue spending towards that $1,500 goal.

When you reactivate your banners, they will receive another share of the spend that would have been evenly distributed across all banners. For instance, with 15 banners and a $1,500 budget, each banner should ideally get $100. If I reactivate them, it means they will each get another $100 in ad spend. Since I’m about $300 short of my target, I may need to turn on another banner to reach that $1,500. 

Once you hit that $1,500, you’ll unlock additional traffic source settings, allowing you to upload new banners and creatives. So, to summarize, if your campaign has turned off or your banners have become inactive before hitting $1,500, reactivate some banners and, if needed, the campaign itself to ensure you reach that minimum spend.
