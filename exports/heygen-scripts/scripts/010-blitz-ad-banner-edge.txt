In this video, I'll show you the importance of testing your ads more than any other part of your marketing funnel. Ads are the first point of contact with potential customers, so they must capture attention, generate interest, and compel action within seconds. Since ads are the most frequently seen component of the funnel, they require the most attention to combat fatigue. The more an ad is seen, the more you need to change it to keep people clicking. Keeping ads fresh is key.

Let’s discuss the impact of ad elements. Ad elements include the angle, headline, image, supporting copy, call to action, and layout. The angle represents the overall idea of the ad. The headline, which you can see in blue, is crucial. For example, "When neuropathy hits, try this" is a strong headline. The image and supporting copy also play significant roles. The layout can be adjusted; for instance, you can move the image or change the text color. The elements are listed in order of impact, with the ad angle being the most critical at 70%. The headline contributes 15%, the image 10%, and supporting copy 5%. 

Get the ad angle right, and everything else will fall into place. Your metrics reflect your marketing. If your marketing is good, the metrics you see in DIYTrax will also look good. Conversely, if your marketing is poor, no amount of tweaking will improve your metrics. Remember, the magic is in the marketing, and 70% of that magic lies in the ad angle.

All marketing is storytelling, and our affiliate funnel tells a story through three steps: the ad, the advertorial presell page, and the offer page. The ad is the beginning of the story, and the ad angle is how all the elements work together to start that story. For example, the ad should create curiosity. If someone is intrigued enough, they will click to learn more. 

When creating ads, focus on the pain points of your audience. Curiosity is essential; you want to keep the loop open. Don't reveal too much information in the ad. Instead, sell the click and the interest in the idea, not just the product. Start with the advertorial and pull ideas from it to create congruence in your ads. The ad angle should be relevant to the next step, which is the advertorial. You can edit the advertorial to fit your needs, ensuring that your ads and landing pages are aligned for maximum effectiveness.
