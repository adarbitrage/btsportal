In this video, I'll show you how to use the Bridge Page Bot to create compelling copy for your landing pages. This bot is similar to the angle architect. You start by providing some demographic information and a transcript. For this demonstration, I’ll use the Nagano tonic transcript, which is completely clean and unrelated to my previous training.

You can keep the demographic details simple, like age, gender, and geolocation. However, feel free to add more complex information if needed. Once you input this data along with the transcript from the VSL, the bot begins to generate content. It will ask you for specific marketing angles you want to use for your bridge page. I’ll pull up my previous chat with angles and copy the target demographic and angle number one to provide to the bot.

The bot will then create a page using the exact headline: "Japanese water trick that's making American women 20 years younger." It will generate copy that aligns with this angle without trying to improve on the original headline. For example, it might ask, "Have you ever wondered why Japanese women look so young and slim?" The copy will highlight how Japanese women maintain their health and vitality well into their later years, thanks to a simple seven-second morning ritual. This ancient secret is now adapted for American women, yielding stunning results.

You can create multiple angles for different landing pages. For instance, you might use a marriage transformation angle, where the story revolves around a woman named Amy who lost weight and revitalized her relationship. Each angle will follow a similar structure but convey a unique message. You can also explore more obscure angles, like a mother-in-law story, where the focus is on overcoming weight-related comments from family.

The bot consistently produces copy that fits a straightforward pattern. You can adjust the font size and layout to suit your needs. The goal is to create simple, effective bridge pages that emphasize the marketing angle that resonates best with your audience. 

As you develop your landing pages, remember to create separate body copy for each angle. It’s recommended to test around ten different angles initially. The headlines and body copy generated by the bot will work together to form your landing pages. If you have any questions about this process, feel free to ask.
