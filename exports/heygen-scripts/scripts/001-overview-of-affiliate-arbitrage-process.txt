In this video, I'll show you the affiliate arbitrage process and how it works. Affiliate arbitrage involves a traffic source where you run ads. You send clicks from those ads to a landing page you control, and then from that landing page to a sales mechanism owned by the offer owner. When someone makes a purchase, the offer owner pays you commissions. Your profit comes from the difference between what you spend on ads and what you earn in commissions. This gap is what we call arbitrage.

Now, let’s talk about our method. We have several unique traffic sources, including email newsletters and display advertising networks, where you can place ad banners. These ads direct clicks to an advertorial, which is an ad designed to look like an editorial piece. From the advertorial, clicks go to an e-commerce product page. When someone buys a product, you earn commissions.

Another option is ClickBank, which operates similarly. You’ll use the same traffic sources and types of ads, directing clicks to a landing page known as a jump page or bridge page. This page is brief and leads to a video sales letter, or VSL, where a video sells the product. When someone clicks "add to cart" and completes the purchase, you earn commissions.

If you're just starting, I recommend MediaMavens. It may be easier since we provide a proven advertorial to begin with. With ClickBank, you’ll need to create your own landing pages. MediaMavens also offers a flat CPA with no clawbacks, chargebacks, or refunds against you, while ClickBank deducts money from your commissions for refunds or chargebacks. Plus, MediaMavens is exclusive to our program, while ClickBank is open to everyone.

Regardless of which network you choose, you'll set up ads that look like display banners and run them on one or more traffic sources. These ads will lead to a landing page, and from there, clicks will go to a sales page. When a purchase is made, you collect commissions. It really is that simple.
