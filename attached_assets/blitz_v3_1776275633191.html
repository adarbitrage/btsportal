Watch This First: What Is Affiliate Arbitrage?
A short overview of how this business model works — start here before anything else
💡 What is Affiliate Arbitrage? (Plain English)
Here's the basic idea: you promote someone else's product using paid ads. When someone clicks your ad, visits a landing page you've created, and then buys the product — you earn a commission. The "arbitrage" part means you're trying to spend less on ads than you make in commissions. Your goal is to find the right combination of ad, landing page, and audience that makes that happen consistently.

Key Terms — Plain English Definitions

You'll see these terms throughout this guide and in the videos. Bookmark this section and refer back to it whenever something is unclear.

Affiliate Network
A marketplace where companies list products they want promoted. You sign up, pick a product, and get a unique link. When someone buys through your link, you get paid. In The Blitz™, your options are: Media Mavens (our own in-house network), ClickBank, Affiliati, and MaxWeb.
Commission / CPA
The money you earn each time someone buys the product you're promoting. "CPA" stands for Cost Per Acquisition — it's the dollar amount you receive per sale. Example: if you earn $50 per sale, your CPA is $50.
Landing Page
The web page someone sees after clicking your ad. Its job is to warm them up and get them interested enough to click through to the product's sales page. You create and control this page.
Advertorial
A type of landing page that reads like an article or editorial — it tells a story or shares information rather than being an obvious advertisement. Used in Media Mavens campaigns.
Jump Page / Bridge Page
A very short landing page — usually just a headline, an image, and a button — that sends visitors directly to a video sales letter (VSL). Used in ClickBank campaigns.
VSL (Video Sales Letter)
A video that sells the product. This is the main selling tool for many ClickBank products. You don't create the VSL — the product owner does. Your job is to get people to watch it.
Traffic Source / Publisher
The advertising platform where your ads run. Think of it like the network that displays your ads to potential buyers. In The Blitz™, you'll work with one of three publishers: Caterpillar, Grasshopper, or Crane.
Native Ad
An ad that blends in with the content around it — it looks like an article recommendation rather than a traditional banner. You upload the headline and image separately, and the platform assembles the ad. Caterpillar runs native ads.
Banner Ad
A traditional display ad — an image with your headline built directly into it. You create the full image as a single file. Grasshopper and Crane run banner ads.
CTR (Click-Through Rate)
The percentage of people who click your ad or landing page. Higher CTR means your ad or page is grabbing attention. Ad CTR = people who clicked the ad. Landing Page CTR = people who clicked through to the product.
ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)
How much money you made back for every dollar you spent on ads. Example: if you spent $1,000 and made $600 in commissions, your ROAS is 60%. Your goal is to eventually reach 100% ROAS (break-even) and then above it (profit).
Hero Shot
The main image on your landing page — the first big visual a visitor sees. It sets the emotional tone for the page and needs to connect with the headline.
Split Testing (A/B Testing)
Showing different versions of your ads or landing pages to different visitors to see which one performs better. You might test 10 different headlines, for example, and see which one generates the most clicks or sales.
DIYTrax
Your campaign tracking dashboard. It connects your ads, landing pages, and affiliate links together, and records which combinations are generating sales. Think of it as your campaign's control center.
Placement
The specific format and size of your ad. On Caterpillar, placements are things like "16:9 image" or "9:16 video." On Grasshopper, they're banner sizes like 300×250 or 970×250.
Compliance
A review process where your ads and landing pages are checked to make sure they follow the rules of the affiliate network and publisher. You must submit your creative assets for compliance approval before going live.

0.1 — Understanding What Your Budget Is Actually Buying

This is the most important mindset shift for new students: your ad spend in The Blitz™ is buying data, not revenue.

Think of it like this: imagine you're a scientist running experiments. Each experiment costs money and teaches you something. The early experiments almost always "fail" in the traditional sense — but they produce information that makes your later experiments far more likely to succeed. That's exactly what testing rounds do in affiliate marketing.

💡 What does "buying data" actually mean?
When you spend $500 in Round 1, you're finding out which of your 10 ad headlines your audience responds to best. That information is incredibly valuable — without it, you'd be guessing. The sales you make in Round 1 are a bonus. The real prize is knowing which headline wins so you can use it in every future round.

If you go in expecting to profit in Round 1, you'll panic and make bad decisions. If you go in knowing a loss is expected, you'll stay calm and make data-driven decisions.

0.2 — The Three Phases of The Blitz™

Every Blitz™ campaign follows the same three-phase sequence. You cannot skip phases or jump ahead — each phase uses what you learned in the previous one.

Phase 1
BUILD
Set up your offer, landing pages, ads, and tracking. Go live.
→
Phase 2
TEST
Run 2–3 rounds of testing. Cut what doesn't work. Keep what does.
→
Phase 3
SCALE
Spend more on what's proven to work. Expand to new placements.
💬 Why this order?
Think of it like baking. You can't frost a cake before it's baked, and you can't bake without a recipe. BUILD gives you the recipe. TEST tells you which version of the recipe your audience actually likes. SCALE means baking that recipe in larger and larger batches now that you know it works.

Phase Gates — You Must Meet These Before Moving On

At the end of each phase, there's a "gate" — a set of criteria you must meet before advancing. These gates exist to protect your money. They prevent you from spending more on something that hasn't proven it can work yet.

✓ Gate to Enter Test Phase
Your campaign is live. Ads are running. Tracking is set up. You are actively spending.
Caterpillar — Gate to Move from Round 1 to Round 2
  • You have spent at least $500
  • You have identified 1 winning headline
Grasshopper / Crane — Gate to Move from Round 1 to Round 2
  • You have earned at least $300 in commissions
  • You have at least 2 banner ads that made sales
✓ Gate to Enter Scale Phase
Rounds 1 and 2 are complete. You've found winning ad + landing page combinations. You've met the Round 2 success targets. (Full details are in Module 2.)
Important: The Test Phase Almost Always Takes Multiple Rounds Most students do not pass through the Test phase on their first attempt at each round. That is completely normal and expected. The whole point of testing is to keep learning and improving until a clear winner emerges. Think of it less like a pass/fail exam and more like a series of experiments — each one gets you closer to the answer. The speed at which you progress through testing depends mainly on how quickly you develop two core skills: writing effective headlines and selecting the right images. The training in Module 1, Steps 2 and 3, directly builds those skills. Students who invest time in that training move through testing faster.

Step 5 — Create Your Ad Creative Everyone

Your "ad creative" is the actual ad that people will see — the headline, image, or banner that gets them to click. What you create depends on your publisher.

✦ Caterpillar — Native Ads
💡 How Caterpillar ads work
Caterpillar is a "native" ad platform. This means your ads look like recommended articles or content — they blend into the page rather than looking like obvious advertisements. You upload three things separately: your headlines (as plain text), one description (as plain text), and one image. The platform assembles the ad automatically. You do not design a complete ad banner.

  • 1 Description — 90 characters or fewer, appears below the headline in the ad
  • 1 Image — 16:9 ratio (landscape/horizontal), minimum size 960×540px, recommended 1280×720px or larger

DIYTrax is the "brain" of your campaign. It sits in the middle between your ads and your affiliate link, and it tracks everything — which ad someone clicked, which landing page they saw, and whether they made a purchase. Without it, you'd have no idea which parts of your campaign are working. Think of it like a flight data recorder for your campaign.

Module 3

Scale — Multiply Your Profits

Scaling is the exciting part — but it only works if you've done the testing properly. You are not introducing new ideas at this stage. You are spending more money on the exact ads and pages that have already proven they work. Think of it like finding a machine that turns $1 into $1.20, then feeding it more and more $1 bills.
You Must Meet ALL of These Before Scaling Rounds 1 and 2 (and Round 3 for Caterpillar) are complete. You have found ad + landing page combinations that are profitable or near-profitable. You have met the success criteria from your last round. Scaling without meeting these criteria is how people lose money.
Method 1

Spend More on What's Working

Increase your daily budget and cost-per-click bids on profitable placements. Remove all non-profitable ads first. Try 2× budget, let it stabilize, then try 5×, then 10×. Example: profiting $50/day at $100 spend → test $200/day, then $500/day.

Method 2

Test New Ad Placements

Take your proven ads and proven landing pages to new placements across publishers. Budget $1,500 minimum per new placement. Testing 2–3 new placements costs $3,000–$4,500. This expands your reach while using only what's already proven to work.

Method 3

Graduate to the Master Publisher

A dedicated email blast to a large, engaged subscriber list. Much bigger reach, higher potential return — but also higher risk. Only attempt after 14+ consecutive days of profitability on your current publishers.

Master Publisher Requirements

The Master publisher works differently from banner and native ads — you're sending a single email to a large list rather than showing ads to browsing visitors. The bar is higher because the stakes are higher. Make sure you've checked all of these before trying it:

  • At least 14 consecutive days of profitable campaigns on Caterpillar, Grasshopper, or Crane
  • Your single best-performing headline + image combination is clearly identified
  • Your single best-performing landing page is clearly identified
  • You have dedicated budget set aside specifically for this test
