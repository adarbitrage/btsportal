# BTS Portal — Guided Onboarding + Accountability Partner System (Build Spec)

## Goal

New members (3-Month and up) have just paid a lot of money and are scared; some have
buyer's remorse. Replace the current 5-step onboarding with a simpler guided
sequence, and build an accountability-partner system: every 3-Month+ member is
assigned a real partner (round-robin) who does recurring 1-on-1 calls for the whole
term, tracked against Blitz progress, with escalation when a mentee wobbles. The
judgment metric is refund/chargeback reduction, made continuously observable via a
daily NMI poller. Everything adapts proven in-portal patterns; the only genuinely
new subsystems are assignment state and NMI event ingestion.

## Locked decisions (source of truth)

- Partners: start with 3, **headcount scales fast**; round-robin assignment;
  reassignment supported (full history carries; new photo/bio replaces old).
- Eligibility: **3-Month and up**. Term expiry cleanly ends everything (portal
  access already stops at expiry — no separate offboarding).
- Check-ins are **calls only** (no partner email/SMS threads). Cadence set jointly
  on the first call: 1x/week up to daily. Cap: **5 calls/day per partner**.
- Booking: **native BTS-designed UI**, GHL as the engine behind the API. Partners'
  availability managed **in GHL directly** (Sandy relays their hours).
- Kickoff call: with a kickoff coach (photo + bio), **not** a partner. Partner
  calendar unlocks **when the kickoff is BOOKED** (not completed). Partner-call
  slots offered only **after the kickoff's scheduled time**.
- Mentee journey: welcome video → ToS acceptance → profile → book kickoff →
  (partner revealed: photo + bio; calendar unlocked) → book first partner call →
  watch 7 Pillars while waiting → first partner call → Blitz Day 1. Checklist
  visible; **full-width banner on call days** with time + call link.
- Blitz is **23 steps** (blitz-hub-step-v2-{1..23}) — all "Day X" displays use 23.
- Escalation to COO/CEO: **3 consecutive no-shows** OR **14 days with no completed
  partner call** while term active (the vanish rule).
- Hiring signal: admin alert when **fleet average utilization ≥80%** (trailing 7 days).
- Reminders: **portal-sent email + SMS** (not GHL workflows).
- Partners: **flat monthly salary** — no per-call/per-mentee counting needed for pay.
- Coaches can see partner notes. Partners see only their own roster.
- NMI refunds/chargebacks: **daily poller** on the existing queryTransaction;
  baseline refund rate supplied by Adam; dashboard shows partnered-cohort rate vs.
  baseline.
- ToS: the portal's click-wrap ToS step **stays** (it is the only acceptance record
  in the system — diagnostic confirmed no upstream capture). The mentorship
  agreement remains external (GHL business process, deliberately out of portal).
- Kickoff/partner call completion + no-show detection: **GHL appointment webhooks**
  (new handler branch). GHL-side event subscription is a flagged config task (Sandy).

## T1 — Partner role (mirror the coach pattern)

**Creates:** PARTNER_ROLE constant + isPartnerRole() in lib/auth (beside COACH_ROLE
:87); PartnerRoute guard (mirror CoachRoute — allows partner role OR admin with a
new `partners:view` permission); rbac touchpoints; sidebar section for partner nav.
Admin permissions: `partners:view` (super_admin, admin, support_agent),
`partners:manage` (super_admin, admin) added to PERMISSION_MATRIX.
**Acceptance:** a user with role=partner reaches /partner/* routes and nothing
else staff-side; admins with partners:view can view partner surfaces; partner role
grants NO member entitlements (entitlements stay product-derived — hard rule).

## T2 — Assignment schema + round-robin (net-new state)

**Creates:**
- `partners` table: id, user_id FK, display_name, photo_url, bio, is_active,
  max_daily_calls (default 5), created_at. (Mirror of coaches table shape.)
- `partner_assignments` table: id, member_id FK, partner_id FK, status
  (active|ended|reassigned), assigned_at, ended_at, ended_reason. **History
  preserved**: reassignment ends the old row and inserts a new one — never
  overwrite. Partial unique index: one ACTIVE assignment per member.
- `assignRoundRobin(memberId)`: picks the active partner with the fewest active
  assignments (ties → least-recently-assigned). Trigger: on grant of any 3-Month+
  product (hook the existing grant path — same spot entitlements resolve).
  Idempotent: skip if an active assignment exists.
- Term-expiry: when the qualifying product expires (existing expiry sweep),
  assignment rows flip to ended. No partner action needed — portal access already
  stops.
- Reassignment: admin action (permission partners:manage) — end current, assign
  named partner or re-run round-robin, reason recorded. Notes/history remain keyed
  to member_id so the new partner inherits everything.
- `kickoff_coaches` table (or a flag on coaches): photo_url + bio for the kickoff
  coach display; round-robin across kickoff coaches at booking time.
**Acceptance:** granting a 3month product creates exactly one active assignment;
round-robin balances counts; reassignment preserves history and swaps the active
row; expiry ends assignments; a 6month direct buyer gets assigned identically.

## T3 — New onboarding contract (replace steps, keep the gate)

**Keeps:** the two-column contract (onboardingStep/onboardingComplete), the four
guards untouched, the PATCH route shape, **and the completion side effects
(email-sequence cancel + nurture enrollment, onboarding.ts:128-133) — carried over
verbatim.**
**Replaces:** step definitions. New steps:
  1 welcome video → 2 ToS acceptance (existing documents plumbing; prerequisite
  check re-wired to new numbering) → 3 profile (existing fields) → 4 book kickoff →
  5 book first partner call → 6 watch 7 Pillars → 7 first partner call done →
  complete → Blitz.
- Steps 4/5/7 are **event-advanced**, not click-advanced: 4 completes on kickoff
  BOOKED (T5), 5 on partner call BOOKED (T5), 7 on partner call COMPLETED (T7
  webhook). Step 6 is click-advanced (mentee confirms they watched — keep it
  simple; Blitz is the real tracked content).
- Members mid-flight in the OLD flow at deploy: map old step → nearest new step
  (1→1, 2→2, 3→3, 4/5→4). Members already onboardingComplete are untouched.
- Old pages (Orientation, QuickStart) removed; Welcome/Documents/Profile adapted.
**Acceptance:** new member walks 1→7; direct-API step skips still blocked by
prerequisites (ToS row required for step 2→3, kickoff booking for 4→5, etc.);
completion fires the same email-sequence handoff as today; a pre-deploy
mid-onboarding member lands on the correct mapped step; completed members see no
change.

## T4 — Partner dashboard

**Creates:** /partner/dashboard (+ /partner/mentees/:id detail), PartnerRoute-gated.
- Roster: active assignments with member name, **Blitz Day X of 23** (via the
  coach-dashboard read pattern — fetchRecentActivity/course_progress), cadence,
  last completed call, next booked call.
- Today view: today's calls with time + **call link** (GHL appointment's meeting
  URL, stored on the local booking record at create time).
- Per-mentee: mark call done / add note / flag concern (notes keyed to member_id;
  visible to coaches via a read surface on the existing coach mentee view);
  cadence setter (free-form times/week 1–7, set after the first call).
- Consecutive no-show counter + "days since last completed call" surfaced per
  mentee (data from T7; display-only here).
**Acceptance:** partner sees ONLY their roster; coach can read partner notes on a
shared mentee; admin with partners:view sees all partners' dashboards; Blitz day
matches the member's actual course_progress.

## T5 — Native booking (kickoff + partner calls), BTS-designed, GHL-backed

**Adapts** the session-pack booking flow end to end (no session-credit deduction —
partner calls aren't credit-metered; kickoff isn't either).
- **Kickoff booking (step 4):** shows the round-robined kickoff coach's photo +
  bio; availability via getFreeSlots on that coach's GHL calendar; book →
  createAppointment → local `call_bookings` record (type=kickoff). On success:
  advance onboarding step 4 AND unlock partner booking.
- **Partner booking (step 5 + ongoing):** shows the assigned partner's photo + bio;
  availability via getFreeSlots on the partner's GHL calendar, **filtered**: (a)
  no slots before the kickoff's scheduled datetime (first booking only), (b) no
  slots on a day where the partner already has 5 booked calls (enforce the cap
  in OUR layer — GHL free-slots won't know the cap). Book/reschedule/cancel all
  in-portal via the GHL client.
- `call_bookings` table: id, member_id, staff_id (partner or kickoff coach), type
  (kickoff|partner), ghl_appointment_id, scheduled_at, meeting_url, status
  (booked|completed|no_show|canceled), created_at. This is the local truth the
  dashboard, reminders, escalations, and checklist all read.
- **Call-day banner:** full-width top bar for the mentee on any day with a booked
  call — "Accountability call today at {time}" + meeting link. (Kickoff day too.)
**Acceptance:** mentee cannot see partner slots before kickoff is booked; first
partner slot offered is after kickoff's scheduled time; a partner with 5 bookings
on a day shows no slots that day; cancel/reschedule round-trips to GHL; banner
shows on call days with a working link; booking events advance onboarding steps
4 and 5.

## T6 — Mentee-facing guided sequence UI

**Creates:** the new onboarding pages/checklist per T3's steps — welcome video
first, checklist beneath ("Step 3 of 7"), each step card states plainly what to do
next. Photo + bio cards for kickoff coach (revealed at step 4) and partner
(revealed the moment kickoff is booked). Post-onboarding: a persistent
"Your Accountability Partner" panel (partner photo/bio, cadence, next call,
check-ins completed) on the dashboard — the relationship status the mentee sees.
**Tone:** calm, one-thing-at-a-time — this flow exists because new members are
scared; never show more than the current step's action. 7 Pillars step links
straight to /core-training/7-pillars.
**Acceptance:** a brand-new 3month member sees exactly one next action at every
point from first login to Blitz Day 1; partner reveal happens at kickoff booking;
checklist state survives logout/login; completed members see the partner panel,
not the checklist.

## T7 — GHL appointment webhooks (completed / no-show / canceled)

**Extends** POST /webhooks/ghl (existing HMAC route) with an appointment-events
branch: match ghl_appointment_id → call_bookings row → update status.
- completed → status=completed (advances onboarding step 7 if it's the first
  partner call; feeds "last completed call").
- no_show → status=no_show (feeds the consecutive counter; counter resets on any
  completed call).
- canceled → status=canceled (does NOT count as a no-show).
- Unmatched appointment ids: log and ignore (other GHL calendars exist).
**Config task (flagged, non-code):** subscribe the GHL location to appointment
status events pointing at /webhooks/ghl — Sandy/admin action; the spec is not done
until a real GHL no-show round-trips into call_bookings.
**Acceptance:** marking an appointment no-show in GHL flips the local row within
the webhook round-trip; completed kickoff/partner calls advance the right state;
canceled ≠ no-show; a replayed webhook is idempotent (status transitions only
forward).

## T8 — Call reminders (email + SMS from the portal)

**Near-copies** scheduled-comms' coaching-call jobs for call_bookings: 24h-before
email + 1h-before SMS, same dedup pattern (checkAndRecordSend). New templates
(partner call + kickoff variants). New per-category opt-in column
`partnerCallSmsOptIn` following the existing pattern, enforced at the single
send-point.
**Acceptance:** booked call → both reminders fire once each at the right offsets;
opt-out suppresses SMS only; canceled/rescheduled bookings don't fire stale
reminders. **Flag:** SMS is env-gated in prod (TWILIO_ACCOUNT_SID) — reminders
must degrade to email-only gracefully until that's set.

## T9 — Escalations + capacity alert (on-call dispatcher)

**Creates** a poll runner (BullMQ, reuse the scheduled-comms cadence) evaluating:
- **3 consecutive no-shows** (per member, from call_bookings; reset on completed)
  → fire on-call alert "Mentee escalation: {name} — 3 consecutive no-shows"
  (email channel — reaches COO/CEO via the armed ops email; PagerDuty/Slack ride
  along if ever configured).
- **Vanish rule:** active 3month+ term AND active assignment AND no completed
  partner call in 14 days (measured from last completed, or from assignment start
  for never-called) → same alert path, distinct message.
- **Fleet capacity:** trailing-7-day booked-slots ÷ available-slots across active
  partners ≥80% → admin alert "Partner capacity ≥80% — time to hire." (Available
  slots derived from GHL free-slots snapshot + the 5/day cap.)
- All three: fire/clear semantics + throttling via the existing dispatcher (no
  re-alert spam); each firing also lands in the System Health alert timeline
  (registry additions per the existing pattern).
**Acceptance:** simulated 3rd consecutive no-show fires exactly one alert and
clears on the next completed call; a member dark 14 days fires the vanish alert;
capacity math verified against a constructed roster; alerts visible in System
Health.

## T10 — NMI refund/chargeback poller + metric view (independent)

**Creates:**
- Daily job on the existing queryTransaction (nmi-gateway.ts:176) querying NMI for
  refund/chargeback-condition transactions since the last poll; match to members
  via order/transaction records; stamp `member_refund_events` (member_id, type
  refund|chargeback, amount_cents, nmi_transaction_id UNIQUE for idempotency,
  occurred_at). Include refunds NOT initiated by us (the current blind spot).
- Admin metric view (admin permission): partnered-cohort refund/chargeback rate
  (members with an assignment) vs. the Adam-supplied baseline (a settings value,
  editable), trended monthly. Optional cut: rate by check-in engagement band.
**Acceptance:** a dashboard-initiated NMI refund (not via our ops flow) appears
within one poll cycle stamped to the right member; re-polls never double-count;
the metric view shows cohort rate vs. baseline. **Scope fence:** read-only
ingestion — this task must not touch charging, refund-issuing, or billing logic.

## Cross-cutting rules

- Partner role grants no entitlements; content access stays product-derived.
- call_bookings is the single local truth for calls — dashboard, banner,
  reminders, escalations, and checklist all read it; GHL is the engine, never the
  store of record.
- All new alerts go through the on-call dispatcher (never ad-hoc email sends).
- Nothing in this build touches the mentorship agreement (external, deliberate)
  or weakens the ToS gate. Pre-existing known holes (legacy members without ToS
  rows; version-static signing) are OUT of scope — documented, not worsened.
- Every schema task ships companion idempotent SQL + drift-baseline update per
  repo convention.
