# BTS Portal — Ad-Spend Funding (Build Spec)

## Goal
One funding page. Member enters a deposit amount ($1,000–$10,000), pays via the
native NMI checkout, and the confirmed charge is credited to their ad-spend balance.
The balance ledger does not exist yet, so this build creates it; your developer's
draw-down debits the same ledger.

## The ledger (the shared contract with your developer)
Two tables. Transactions are the source of truth; balance is their sum.

- `ad_spend_transactions` — one row per money movement:
  `id`, `user_id`, `amount_cents` (positive = credit, negative = debit), `type`
  (`funding` | `spend`), `source` (e.g. `nmi`), `nmi_transaction_id` (nullable, for
  funding rows — **unique**, for idempotency), `created_at`, optional `note`.
- Current balance = `SUM(amount_cents)` for the member. Expose it as a single
  read (`GET /api/ad-spend/balance`).

Funding writes `type='funding'` credit rows. Draw-down (your dev) writes
`type='spend'` debit rows. One table, two writers.

## Funding flow
1. **Page** (`/ad-spend/fund` or similar): member enters an amount; validate
   $1,000–$10,000 client-side; on submit, charge via the existing NMI checkout for
   that amount.
2. **Charge**: use the native NMI billing system already built — one-time charge of
   the requested amount.
3. **Credit on confirmation**: when NMI confirms the charge, insert a `funding`
   credit row for the **amount NMI reports as charged** (not the requested amount),
   keyed by `nmi_transaction_id`.
4. **Display**: member sees their current balance (the SUM) on the funding page.

## Hard rules
- **Enforce $1,000–$10,000 server-side** in the charge/credit handler, not just the
  page. Reject out-of-bounds amounts even if a manipulated request reaches the
  server. This gates real money.
- **Credit = NMI-confirmed charged amount**, never the requested amount. Request
  drives the charge; the confirmed charge drives the credit.
- **Idempotent**: dedup on `nmi_transaction_id` so a repeated confirmation/webhook
  never double-credits.
- **Not an entitlement product.** This grants no entitlement keys, gets no rank in
  the level engine, and never appears in the content-access map's product columns.
  Funding ad spend must not gate content or change a member's level. (Model it as a
  funding flow, not a `products` row. If a catalog/reporting record is wanted later,
  add a product of `type='funding'` that grants nothing.)

## Acceptance
- Depositing $2,500 charges $2,500 via NMI and adds a $2,500 credit; balance
  reflects it.
- A request for $500 or $50,000 is rejected server-side.
- A member's level and content access are unchanged after funding.
- Replaying the NMI confirmation for one transaction credits once, not twice.
- Balance = sum of all funding credits minus draw-down debits (once your dev writes
  spend rows).

## Handoff
The ledger schema above is the contract. Your developer builds the draw-down to
insert `type='spend'` debit rows against `ad_spend_transactions` and reads balance
the same way. Nothing else on the funding side depends on their work.
