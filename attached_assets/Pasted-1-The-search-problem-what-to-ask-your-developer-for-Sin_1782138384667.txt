1. The search problem — what to ask your developer for
Since it's flat files/CMS without a real database, the search is probably running client-side or through a simple indexed search library (think Lunr.js, Algolia, Fuse.js, or a basic CMS plugin). The fix isn't re-architecting anything — it's a matching mode change. Specifically, ask your developer to check/change:

Switch from AND/phrase matching to OR matching with relevance scoring. Every good search tool (Algolia, Fuse.js, Lunr, Elasticsearch) supports this — it's usually one config flag, not a rebuild. The fix is making sure individual keyword matches still surface results, ranked by relevance, rather than requiring all words to match closely together.
Add fuzzy matching / typo tolerance if not already on — catches "starting" matching "start," "started," "getting started."
Check what fields are actually indexed. If search only looks at title + a one-line description, that explains weak results. Ask whether body content, tags, and phase/step metadata are included in the search index.
Weight title matches higher than body matches so the most relevant lesson surfaces first instead of getting buried.

This is worth raising as its own ticket to whoever handles your changes — it's likely a quick fix once someone looks at the search config, and it'll have the single biggest impact on usability since it affects every search a mentee runs.
2. The navigation/design problem — what you can likely do yourself
Even with great search, 100+ items needs browse paths for people who don't know what to search for yet. Right now the page only offers a search box — there's no way to browse by category without typing something first. A few concrete additions:

Show popular/featured categories or starting points by default, before anyone types anything — e.g. "New to BTS? Start here" with links to Lesson 1-3, or cards for each Phase.
Use your existing filter tags as a browsable grid, not just post-search filters. Blitz Guide, Resource Library, Glossary, Apps & Tools, FAQ, Training, Coaching — turn these into clickable cards on the landing page itself.
Surface your Blitz phase structure (Phase 1, 2, 3 — I can see "Phase 2 — Test" in your results) as its own browsing hierarchy, since mentees will often know their phase before they know the exact lesson name.
